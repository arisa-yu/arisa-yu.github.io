## Overview of this accessibilty flare project
For my Flare project, I created a guide that explains why web accessibility matters and how audio description can make videos more inclusive. The project focuses on helping writers and content creators understand their role in accessibility, especially in digital spaces where most learning now happens. I covered key ideas such as the medical and social models of disability, universal design, and the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines. The main focus of the guide is audio description, including what it is, who benefits from it, and how to add it to videos using ClipChamp. I also included tips for writing effective descriptions and using integrated description when recording tutorials or demos. Overall, the goal of my project was to show that accessibility is not just an extra feature but a responsibility when creating content.

### Tools and concepts used
I used MadCap Flare to organize the content into a help-style format with headings, sections, and step-by-step instructions. I also relied on accessibility concepts such as universal design, WCAG principles (perceivable, operable, understandable, and robust), and integrated audio description. ClipChamp was used as an example tool for recording and editing audio descriptions. In addition, I applied technical writing strategies like clear language, organization hierarchy, and descriptive explanations to make the information easy to follow.

### Top 3 things I learned from this project so far  
1. Accessibility benefits everyone, and not just for people with permanent disabilities.
2. Designing with accessibility in mind from the start is more effective than adding it in later.
3. Writers play a major role in accessibility, and not soley designers or programmers.
