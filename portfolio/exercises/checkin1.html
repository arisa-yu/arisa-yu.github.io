Save a Life and Start Fostering a Furry Friend!

If you're not ready to adopt, fostering is a great way to open your home and heart to a companion while making their lives a little easier.

Why it is important to foster

  1. It reduces euthanasia.
  2. It helps make space for more animals in shelters.
  3. Fostering helps animals learn how to socialize in home settings and with other people.
  4. It reduces disease spread since they are in a cleaner environment.
  5. Fostering also benefits you since you're doing a good deed and made a friend along the way!

Where you can find places to foster

A quick google search followed by your area should provide you with many selections. However, here are some popular shelters that could be a good place to start:

  • ASPCA
  • The Massachusetts Humane Society
  • North Shore Animal League America

My personal experience

I fostered a 2 year old dog in 2018, and had such a wonderful experience! Junebug, a coonhound mix, stayed with us for about a month before being adopted!
